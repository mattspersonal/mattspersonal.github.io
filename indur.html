Vocabulary Definitions

Garrulous

Excessively talkative, especially on trivial matters.

Endemic

Regularly found among particular people or in a certain area.

Banal

So lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring.

Obdurate

Stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action.

Altruistic

Showing a selfless concern for the well-being of others; unselfish.

Germane

Relevant to a subject under consideration.

Succinct

Briefly and clearly expressed.

Elucidate

To make something clear; to explain.

Intrinsic

Belonging naturally; essential.

Obstreperous

Noisy and difficult to control.

Catharsis

The process of releasing strong or repressed emotions.

Pariah

An outcast.

Laconic

Using very few words.

Pithy

Concise and full of meaning.

Mendacity

Untruthfulness.

Alacrity

Brisk and cheerful readiness.

Incorrigible

Not able to be corrected or reformed.

Assiduous

Showing great care and perseverance.

Immutable

Unchanging over time or unable to be changed.

Sallow

(Of a person's face or complexion) of an unhealthy yellow or pale brown color.

Fractious

Irritable and quarrelsome.

Aggrandize

Increase the power, status, or wealth of.

Dilatory

Slow to act; intended to delay.

Esoteric

Intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people.

Extraneous

Irrelevant or unrelated to the subject being dealt with.

Deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.

Archaic

Very old or old-fashioned.

Taciturn

Reserved or uncommunicative in speech; saying little.

Supercilious

Behaving or looking as though one thinks one is superior to others.

Recalcitrant

Having an obstinately uncooperative attitude toward authority or discipline.

Egregious

Outstandingly bad; shocking.

Abstruse

Difficult to understand; obscure.

Impetuous

Acting or done quickly and without thought or care.

Insipid

Poignant

Evoking a keen sense of sadness or regret.

Capitulate

Cease to resist an opponent; surrender.

Insidious

Proceeding in a gradual, subtle way, but with harmful effects.

Incipient

Spurious

Intrepid

Provincial

Mellifluous

Ribald

Bucolic

Relating to the pleasant aspects of the countryside and country life.

Pastoral

Concerning or appropriate to the giving of spiritual guidance; or, relating to rural life.

Irreverent

Showing a lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously.

Pious

Devoutly religious.

Convivial

Friendly, lively, and enjoyable.

Obsequious

Obedient or attentive to an excessive or servile degree.

Friendly, good-natured, or easy to talk to.

Impudent

Languid

Displaying or having a disinclination for physical exertion or effort; slow and relaxed.

Indignant

Feeling or showing anger or annoyance at what is perceived as unfair treatment.

Pedantic
